Hello, Switzerland!

Our adventures in Switzerland

Hallo! We are Hansli & Heidi the adventurous bunnies who love to hop around the world. We have already visited Switzerland twice, because our mother (the author of this blog) has two big loves in her heart — St. Bernard dogs and Swiss chocolate. That’s why she took us on her dream travel, and of course we happily hopped along!

Barryland in Martigny

In Martigny we met the most famous Swiss dogs — the gentle giants called St. Bernards. They told us about their brave history in the snowy Alps, where they once saved travelers from avalanches and storms. We even visited the Barryland Museum, where we met the dogs in real life. Imagine: fluffy, giant friends with kind eyes, wagging tails, and warm hearts — we were completely in love!

The most famous foods from Switzerland

After meeting the St. Bernards, we had to taste Switzerland’s other treasure… yes, chocolate! Sweet, creamy, and so delicious that our whiskers were covered in cocoa dust. No wonder our mother couldn’t resist bringing us here again and again.

But Switzerland is not only about chocolate — there are so many tasty dishes to try! Here are the most famous of them:

  • Fondue – Melted cheese served in a pot, where you dip bread pieces using long forks. A cozy dish, perfect for sharing.

  • Raclette – Melted cheese scraped over boiled potatoes, pickles, and sometimes cured meats.

  • Rösti – A crispy potato dish, similar to hash browns, originally from the German-speaking part of Switzerland.
Bunnies’ Pick: Fondue

Fondue — a big pot of melted cheese where we dip bread with our little paws. For us, it’s the cheesiest adventure in Switzerland!

Interesting facts about Switzerland

Switzerland, with its majestic mountains, delicious chocolate, and charming furry friends, has become one of our favorite destinations in the world! Its warm hospitality, stunning nature, and tasty cuisine make it truly unique. We have also gathered some interesting facts about Switzerland:

  1. Four languages in one country – Switzerland has four official languages: German, French, Italian, and Romansh. Depending on where you travel, street signs, food, and even greetings can change!

  2. Home of chocolate – The Swiss eat more chocolate per person than any other country in the world. On average, each person eats about 10 kg of chocolate a year.

  3. The land of mountains - The Alps cover about 60% of Switzerland’s land area. No wonder Switzerland is famous for skiing, hiking, and breathtaking mountain views.

  4. No capital city? – Technically, Switzerland doesn’t have an official capital. Bern is considered the “federal city,” but it’s not written in the constitution as the capital.

  5. Where the Red Cross was born – The famous humanitarian organization, the International Committee of the Red Cross, was founded in Geneva in 1863.

Memories That Stay

As we returned home, we couldn’t stop thinking about our Swiss adventure. What we remembered most were the smiles of the locals, their kindness, hugs with Saint Bernards, the taste of local cheese and bread, and the magical mountains all around. Switzerland is truly a place that stays in your heart and makes you want to come back again and again.
