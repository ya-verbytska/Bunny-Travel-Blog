Hi!
We are Lars & Freja!

We love calm mornings, cozy blankets, and candlelight dinners — so no wonder we fell in love with Denmark, the home of hygge!

We spent four unforgettable days in Copenhagen, exploring palaces, museums, and charming streets. Denmark quickly became one of our favorite places — peaceful, stylish, and full of kindness.

Royal adventures

Our journey began at the Amalienborg Palace, where the Danish royal family lives. We watched the guards change — such precision and grace!

Then we visited the Christiansborg Palace, where history meets elegance. Walking through the grand halls, we imagined royal banquets and the echo of carriages arriving outside.

Copenhagen’s palaces truly made us feel like royal bunnies for a day!

Design, architecture & the spirit of hygge

Next, we hopped into the Design Museum and the Danish Architecture Center. What amazed us most was how modern technology and coziness blend perfectly in Danish life.

We also visited the Royal Library, known as The Black Diamond. We wanted to read something there, but Danish turned out to be quite a challenge for two little bunnies! So instead, we enjoyed the peaceful atmosphere, admired the view over the water, and loved how cozy and modern the library felt inside.

Floating and fun

We couldn’t miss a boat tour around Copenhagen’s canals — what a view! Colorful houses, happy faces, and the gentle sound of water surrounded us.

Later, we spent a cheerful evening in Tivoli Gardens, one of the world’s oldest amusement parks. We rode the carousel, watched twinkling lights, and shared cotton candy.

The Carlsberg experience

We also visited the Carlsberg Brewery, one of the oldest and most famous in Europe. There we learned how beer is brewed — from golden barley to sparkling bubbles — and discovered the long, proud history behind the brand.

The mix of tradition, craftsmanship, and innovation truly impressed us.

The taste of Denmark

Denmark’s food is a blend of tradition and simplicity, full of cozy flavors and local ingredients.

Here are some of the country’s most famous dishes we learned about:

  • Smørrebrød – Open-faced rye sandwiches topped with fish, meat, or cheese — a true Danish classic.

  • Frikadeller – Small, juicy meatballs often served with potatoes and gravy.

  • Stegt flæsk – Crispy pork with parsley sauce, sometimes called Denmark’s national dish.

  • Æbleskive – Little round pancakes, soft inside and sprinkled with sugar, usually enjoyed at Christmas.

Bunnies’ Pick: Cheesecake in Copenhagen

Even though cheesecake isn’t a traditional Danish dish, for us it became the taste of Copenhagen! We found a cozy café Bertel's Salon with the best cheesecakes we’ve ever tried — creamy, delicate, and full of happiness. We sampled several flavors (just to be sure!) and agreed: “Denmark, your cheesecake is pure hygge!”

Interesting facts about Denmark

Denmark may be small, but it’s full of surprises! From royal traditions to modern design and cozy hygge moments, this charming country has a story behind every corner. Here are some fun facts we discovered during our stay — and they made us love Denmark even more!

A kingdom with one of the oldest monarchies

The Danish royal family can trace its roots back over 1,000 years.

Bicycles everywhere

In Copenhagen, there are more bikes than cars — even the royal family cycles sometimes!

Home of LEGO

The famous toy brand was born in Denmark in 1932. “LEGO” comes from “leg godt,” meaning “play well.”

Green energy leader

Denmark produces nearly half of its electricity from wind power.

Tivoli inspired Disneyland

Walt Disney visited Tivoli Gardens before creating Disneyland — he was enchanted by its atmosphere.

The happiest country in the world

Denmark often ranks among the top happiest nations — maybe because of hygge and work-life balance!

Memories that stay

As we returned home, we carried a piece of hygge in our hearts. We loved absolutely everything — from royal palaces to soft candlelight, from design museums to the calmness of Danish streets.

We brought many hygge ideas into our own home — soft blankets, warm lamps, and quiet moments together.

Now we can’t wait to explore more corners of Denmark — the land of coziness, kindness, and calm.
