Nice to meet you, Miffy

A tiny bunny with a big heart

This is Miffy, a small little bunny. She is curious, friendly and brave. She is very popular in the Netherlands and all over the world — not only among children but also among adults. She may look simple and cute, but her story is deep and touched me to the bottom of my heart.

Miffy was created by the Dutch illustrator Dick Bruna, when he told his son stories about a little bunny. Then he illustrated this character and developed a series of books for children. I was really surprised to find out that Miffy was “born” in 1955, and this year she celebrated her 70th birthday.

My discovery before the trip

Before my travel to the Netherlands, I was familiar with Miffy (but didn’t know her personal story) and thought that this character was probably created in Asia (maybe because of the simple form). But you can imagine how surprised I was when, before my trip to Utrecht and researching places to visit, I found out that there is a Miffy Museum in Utrecht.

Then I started to go deeper and discovered that Dick Bruna (Miffy’s author) was from Utrecht, and he lived and worked in his studio there. And for me, as a big bunny fan and a self-taught illustrator, it was a sign that I definitely must go there to get inspired and feel Miffy’s vibe.

The real name of the little star

An interesting fact is that “Miffy” is not the original name of the bunny. The original name is Nijntje — short for Konijntje (meaning “bunny” in Dutch). But for international readers this name was quite difficult, so “Miffy” was created too.

Dick Bruna drew Miffy only by hand until the end of his life. Even when modern technologies were available, he put a piece of his soul and his great talent into every Miffy masterpiece — and that is wonderful.

The pride of a whole nation

While walking through the city of Utrecht, I was impressed by how famous and beloved Miffy is. Many sculptures, souvenirs, sweets in the shape of Miffy — and even a Miffy traffic light! My inner child was thrilled every time we met a Miffy on our way, and I took a lot of photos with her.

I was very happy to visit Utrecht and the Netherlands. It was also a great example of how a country can love, highlight and preserve its heritage. I met Miffy not only in Utrecht, but also in The Hague’s miniature park, in Gouda, and on lots of postcards and souvenirs. And for me it shows how proud the Dutch are to have her as a part of their story.

Why she touches us all

But what makes Miffy so popular and famous? I don’t know the exact answer, but I think it is her simplicity and cuteness. She is not just a character — she lives a typical child’s life in her stories: full of happiness and fear, challenges, discovering the world, making mistakes and growing from them. Like every child. Like every adult. And that makes her unique and familiar at the same time.

Thank you, Miffy, for being a part of my journey, for helping my inner child reflect and make adult conclusions, for your huge bunny heart, and for reminding me who I really am.
