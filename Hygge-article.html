The Secret of Danish Hygge

What does hygge mean to me

Knitted sweater, warm socks, candles, reading a book near the fireplace, a cup of tea, an atmosphere of coziness and simple happiness in simple things — when you feel calm, good, and relaxed.

And the first place where I want to be when I think about real Hygge is Copenhagen. As far as I know, this term has its roots in Denmark, and the Danes are the masters of it. During my four days in Copenhagen, I tried to understand what the secret of this comfort and well-being is — and the answer was right on the surface. But more about that later.

The City of True Hygge

Can you guess how many days of our whole vacation were rainy? All of them. But to be honest, it didn’t spoil the experience. The rain belongs to Denmark and even adds more charm to it.

Despite the raincoat and the constant drizzle, the feeling of coziness stayed with me. I couldn’t understand why, because I’m definitely not a fan of rain — and in Germany, this weather would annoy me. But not here.

The Danish Way of Life

Enjoying long strolls through the streets, cups of coffee, Danish pastries (so delicious!), the best cheesecake in Copenhagen, boat tours, and visits to museums and palaces — I paid attention to the details. The Danes are masters at making everything cozy: every corner, every detail gives you a feeling of well-being, a sense of safety, and the thought that the worst thing that could happen is simply getting wet from the rain. That was exactly what I need in my everyday life.

The Danes are very relaxed — that was my impression. Of course, they also feel the whole spectrum of emotions, from anger to joy, but in the end, they have this ability to take things easy. And I admire that. They are friendly, maybe a bit reserved in showing emotions, but for me as an introverted person, that’s only a benefit. They appreciate your interest in their culture and are ready to help you or make your experience in Denmark even better.

I think they have learned how to enjoy life independently of external factors and how to create a small cozy corner — an island of safety and comfort. And that’s the main secret.

My Hygge Lesson

I’m not sure that socks, sweaters, candles, or cups alone can help you reach this goal. Hygge is about the ability to live in the moment, to enjoy the little things, and to be happy regardless of the weather. And if socks or a sweater can help you with that — then you should definitely use them to make your life a little bit more Hygge.
