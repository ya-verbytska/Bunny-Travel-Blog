Not only about baguettes and croissants

Paris beyond clichés

What are the first associations you have with France? Maybe crispy pastry, sophisticated style, a red beret, excellent perfume, or delicious cuisine? Or perhaps arrogant people who love their native language so much that they refuse to answer you, even when you’re just a tourist speaking English?

Some of these myths are so deeply rooted in popular culture that when you face the reality, it’s hard to believe things aren’t quite as you imagined them for years.

My highlights of Paris are endless. I was impressed, fascinated and in love. Actually, I didn’t have big expectations before my trip, just because I didn’t want to be disappointed. But my expectations were exceeded — over, over, and over again. And that was amazing.

Live, love, eat pastry

Speaking about stereotypes, I can confirm that the pastries in Paris are indeed incredible. Even if you’re not a big fan, it’s impossible not to visit a cozy boulangerie or pâtisserie and taste the authentic flavors of France. Funny enough, during my four days in Paris, I didn’t try an original croissant! I had quiches, baguettes, tarts, and many sweet masterpieces — but the true croissant I left for my next trip.

But it’s not only about pastries — the entire cuisine was delicious. To describe all the shades and tastes of Parisian food, I probably need to write a separate article.

A little bit of red charm

To be honest, Parisians impressed me with their sense of style. It seems that even in everyday life, they pay great attention to accessories and elegant clothes, and I really admire that. I always have to make an effort to look stylish, but they do it so naturally. The red beret, however, is more of a tourist accessory than a traditional Parisian item. It was easy to distinguish groups of tourists in red berets from real Parisian women — and it was quite funny.

I also succumbed to the charm, but a beret suits me like skis suit a horse! So I limited myself to red lipstick instead and treated myself to a birthday gift — a Gucci red lipstick from Galeries Lafayette. I was very happy about it, even if it was a bit girly.

When “Bonjour” was enough

Now to the language stereotypes. I had heard a lot and was honestly worried about communication, since my French ends at “Je m’appelle Jana” and “Je suis beaucoup voyageuse.” But I had no problems at all — everyone except one person spoke English with me, and I didn’t feel any unfriendliness. Even the person who didn’t speak English tried her best to help us, and others stepped in to translate. So I hope the myth about arrogant Parisians is just that — a myth. Or maybe I was simply lucky.

Returning for the Croissant

What do I think about Paris? It’s charming, unique, and absolutely delicious. Cozy, artistic, and stylish. It has so many layers that I want to come back again and again to explore them all — and of course, to finally taste that original croissant.
